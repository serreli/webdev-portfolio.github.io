Half Marathon

What is a half marathon?

A half marathon, also known as a 21K or 13.1-mile race, is a long-distance running event that covers half the distance of a full marathon. Specifically, a half marathon is 13.1 miles or 21.0975 kilometers long. Half marathons have gained popularity as a challenging yet accessible race distance, appealing to both seasoned runners and those looking to step up from shorter races. They provide a significant physical challenge without requiring the extensive training commitment of a full marathon, making them a popular choice for many runners. Participants in half marathons often find the distance to be a good balance between endurance and speed, offering a test of stamina while being more manageable for those with busy schedules or relatively less running experience. Like marathons, half marathons can be organized as competitive races, charity events, or as part of larger running festivals, fostering a sense of community and accomplishment among participants.
